03 - Biology

CEE Biology syllabus, important topics, and questions

Latest syllabus outline

  • Zoology: evolutionary biology, animal diversity, tissues and histology, selected animals, human physiology, microbial diseases and immunology, medical technology, environment and conservation.
  • Botany: basic component of life, biodiversity, ecology and vegetation, cell biology, genetics, plant anatomy, plant physiology, and developmental botany.

Important topics

  • Human physiology: heart, ECG, respiration, nephron, ADH, endocrine feedback, nerves, reproduction, immunity.
  • Animal tissue recognition and histology clues.
  • Biotechnology, diagnostics, reproductive technology, sterilization, public-health biology.

Practice questions with answers and solutions

Which structure normally acts as the pacemaker of the human heart?

Answer: Sinoatrial node

The SA node has the fastest spontaneous depolarization rate, so it initiates the normal cardiac impulse.

Most carbon dioxide is transported in human blood in which form?

Answer: Bicarbonate ions

Carbonic anhydrase in RBCs converts CO2 to bicarbonate; this is the major transport form.

ADH increases water reabsorption mainly by acting on which part of the nephron?

Answer: Collecting duct

ADH promotes aquaporin insertion in the collecting duct, concentrating urine and conserving water.

Practice questions with answers and solutions

A plane mirror is rotated by 10 degrees. By how much does the reflected ray rotate?

Answer: 20 degrees

When the mirror turns by theta, the reflected ray turns by 2 theta.

For glass of refractive index 1.5 to air, what is the critical angle approximately?

Answer: 41.8 degrees

Use sin C = 1 / 1.5 = 0.6667, so C is about 41.8 degrees.

A coin is 12 cm below water of refractive index 4/3. What is the apparent depth?

Answer: 9 cm

Apparent depth = real depth / refractive index = 12 / (4/3) = 9 cm.

CEE Chemistry syllabus, important topics, and questions

Latest syllabus outline

  • Physical chemistry: stoichiometry, atomic structure, periodic classification, equilibrium, volumetric analysis, ionic equilibrium, electrochemistry, kinetics and bonding.
  • Inorganic, organic, applied and analytical chemistry, including named reactions, functional-group tests, manufacturing processes and biomolecule tests.

Important topics

  • Mole concept, concentration terms, equilibrium, electrochemistry, reaction kinetics and chemical bonding.
  • Haloalkanes, alcohols, phenols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, amines and Grignard reagent.
  • Qualitative analysis, titration indicators, separation methods and functional-group tests.

Practice questions with answers and solutions

In Lucas test at room temperature, which alcohol gives immediate turbidity?

Answer: tert-Butyl alcohol

Tertiary alcohols form stable carbocations and react rapidly with Lucas reagent.

Finkelstein reaction prepares alkyl iodides using which reagent?

Answer: NaI in acetone

NaI converts alkyl chloride or bromide to iodide, while NaCl or NaBr precipitates in acetone.

Ethanol heated with concentrated H2SO4 at 443 K mainly gives what?

Answer: Ethene

At high temperature, concentrated sulphuric acid dehydrates ethanol intramolecularly to ethene.

Practice questions with answers and solutions

Find the next term: 5, 9, 13, 17, ?

Answer: 21

The common difference is 4, so add 4 to 17.

If DOCTOR is coded by shifting every letter two places forward, what is NURSE?

Answer: PWTUG

N to P, U to W, R to T, S to U, and E to G.

Length increases by 15% and breadth by 25%. What is the percentage increase in area?

Answer: 43.75%

New area factor = 1.15 x 1.25 = 1.4375, so increase is 43.75%.

Series: 3, 6, 12, 24, ?

Answer: 48

Each term is multiplied by 2.

If EAST is coded as FB TU by shifting each letter one step forward, how is WEST coded?

Answer: XFTU

W to X, E to F, S to T, and T to U.

A student scores 72 out of 90. What is the percentage?

Answer: 80%

Percentage = 72 / 90 x 100 = 80.

CEE preparation FAQ

Is CEE Biology only Zoology?

Answer: No.

CEE Biology includes both Zoology and Botany, with major weight on human physiology, biodiversity, genetics, plant physiology, ecology and applied biology.

Which CEE chapters should I revise first?

Answer: Start with high-yield repeated chapters.

Prioritize human physiology, optics, organic conversions, equilibrium, electrochemistry, plant physiology, genetics and MAT reasoning.
