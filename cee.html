Exam overview, pattern and preparation route

Use this page as a working dashboard. Official notices can change, so confirm dates, eligibility and final syllabus from MEC before submitting an application.

What CEE covers

CEE / MECEE-BL is the common entrance route for bachelor level medical and allied health science programs in Nepal, including MBBS, BDS, BSc Nursing and related programs depending on the official notice.

Exam pattern

Practice with single-best-answer MCQs, four options per question, a three-hour full mock, negative marking for wrong responses, and percentile-based competition mindset.

Eligibility note

Check MEC notices for program-specific GPA, subject and document rules. Keep citizenship, transcript, equivalence and photo/signature files ready before form season.

Important dates

Placeholder: Application opening, last date, admit card, exam day, result and matching dates should be updated from the current MEC notice as soon as it is published.

Preparation tips

  • Start with the syllabus table, then solve topic-wise MCQs the same day.
  • Keep a wrong-answer notebook for formulas, reactions, definitions and diagrams.
  • Run one timed mixed set every week, then increase to two full mocks near exam time.
  • Use MAT as a score stabilizer: short daily reasoning drills beat last-week cramming.

02 - Physics // $ open physics/

Physics chapters, formulas and model questions

Chapter list

  • Physical quantities, vectors and dimensional analysis.
  • Kinematics, Newton's laws, work-energy, circular motion, gravitation and rotation.
  • Elasticity, surface tension, viscosity, fluid pressure and Bernoulli principle.
  • Heat, calorimetry, thermodynamics and kinetic theory.
  • Wave motion, sound, interference, diffraction, polarization and optics.
  • Electrostatics, capacitors, current electricity, magnetism, EMI and AC.
  • Photoelectric effect, atomic physics, nuclear physics, logic gates and semiconductors.

Objectives and weightage

Target conceptual clarity plus fast substitution. Mechanics, electricity, optics and modern physics usually deserve repeated mixed practice because they produce both direct and numerical MCQs.

MechanicsOpticsElectricityModern

Important formulas

  • v = u + at, s = ut + 1/2 at^2, v^2 = u^2 + 2as.
  • F = ma, W = F s cos theta, P = W/t, KE = 1/2 mv^2.
  • Mirror and lens: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u, magnification = v/u.
  • Critical angle: sin C = 1/mu. Apparent depth = real depth / mu.
  • Ohm's law: V = IR. Capacitor: C = Q/V. Transformer: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np.

Frequently asked topics

  • Ray optics, total internal reflection and apparent depth.
  • Projectile motion and circular motion numericals.
  • Equivalent resistance, meter bridge and potentiometer.
  • Photoelectric equation, de Broglie wavelength and half-life.

03 - Chemistry // $ open chemistry/

Chemistry syllabus, reactions and model questions

Physical chemistry

  • Mole concept, equivalent weight, concentration terms and gas laws.
  • Atomic structure, periodic properties, chemical bonding and molecular structure.
  • Chemical equilibrium, ionic equilibrium, pH, buffer and solubility product.
  • Electrochemistry, chemical kinetics, thermochemistry and redox titration.

Organic chemistry

  • IUPAC, isomerism, hydrocarbons, haloalkanes, alcohols, phenols and ethers.
  • Aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, amines, diazonium salts and biomolecules.
  • Reaction mechanisms: SN1, SN2, elimination, electrophilic substitution and addition.

Inorganic and analytical chemistry

  • Periodic trends, s-block, p-block, transition metals and coordination basics.
  • Qualitative analysis, group reagents, flame tests, indicators and separation methods.
  • Industrial chemistry, fertilizers, cement, glass, polymers and environmental chemistry.

Important reactions and formulas

  • Williamson synthesis: sodium alkoxide + primary haloalkane gives ether.
  • Lucas test: tertiary alcohol gives immediate turbidity.
  • Nernst equation, first-order half-life, pH = -log[H+].
  • Grignard reagent converts carbonyl compounds into alcohols after hydrolysis.

Botany, Zoology and applied biology

Botany

  • Biomolecules, cell structure, enzymes, cell cycle and cell division.
  • Plant diversity, morphology, anatomy and secondary growth.
  • Photosynthesis, respiration, mineral nutrition, transpiration and growth hormones.
  • Genetics, molecular basis of inheritance, biotechnology, ecology and vegetation.

Zoology

  • Animal diversity, evolution, tissues, histology and selected animal types.
  • Digestive, respiratory, circulatory, excretory, nervous, endocrine and reproductive systems.
  • Immunity, microbes, parasites, disease prevention and public health biology.
  • Medical technology, diagnostics, assisted reproduction and environmental biology.

Important concepts

  • Heart conduction, ECG, blood groups, oxygen transport and nephron function.
  • Hormone feedback loops, synapse transmission, muscles and immunity.
  • Mendelian ratios, DNA replication, transcription, translation and mutation.
  • Photosynthesis pigments, C3/C4 plants, transpiration pull and plant hormones.

05 - MAT // $ open mat/

Mental Agility Test topics and drills

Syllabus areas

  • Logical reasoning: syllogism, Venn diagrams, statements and conclusions.
  • Numerical ability: percentage, ratio, average, speed, time and probability basics.
  • Analytical reasoning: arrangements, ranking, coding-decoding and direction sense.
  • Pattern recognition: series, analogy, odd one out, cubes, paper folding and spatial relation.
  • Data interpretation: tables, charts, caselets and quick approximation.

Scoring strategy

MAT rewards daily repetition. Keep a 15-minute reasoning slot and track accuracy. Do not over-invest in one puzzle during a full mock; mark, move and return if time remains.

08 - Notes // $ open notes/

Fast revision notes and checklists

Physics quick notes

  • Write dimensions before using a new formula.
  • For optics, draw the incident ray, normal and refracted/reflected ray first.
  • Memorize common constants and unit conversions.

Chemistry quick notes

  • Keep a one-page organic conversion map.
  • Separate tests by gas, precipitate, color and smell.
  • Practice pH, Ksp, Nernst and first-order kinetics numericals.

Biology quick notes

  • Revise diagrams with labels, not paragraphs only.
  • Link each hormone to gland, target, effect and deficiency/excess.
  • For genetics, write genotype ratios before selecting options.

MAT quick notes

  • Use elimination first, exact calculation second.
  • For series, test difference, ratio, alternating pattern and squares/cubes.
  • For arrangements, draw a compact table before guessing.

Repeated chapters and high-scoring areas

Physics trend

Biology trend

Weightage log

11 - Mock Tests // $ run full-mock

Mock-test structure

Full mock mode

Use 200 questions in 180 minutes when your syllabus pass is complete. Attempt in one sitting, then spend at least two hours reviewing wrong answers.

Mini mock mode

Use 40 questions in 36 minutes for weekday practice: 10 Physics, 10 Chemistry, 10 Biology and 10 MAT or mixed weak chapters.

Mock review template

  • Wrong because of concept gap: revise notes and solve five same-topic MCQs.
  • Wrong because of careless reading: underline negative words and units.
  • Wrong because of time: re-solve without looking at the answer and record seconds taken.
  • Guessed correctly: still review, because lucky answers do not count as mastery.

14 - FAQ // $ cat faq.md

CEE preparation FAQ

What is CEE or MECEE-BL?

CEE / MECEE-BL is the common entrance examination for bachelor level medical education programs in Nepal. Always follow MEC notices for the current application and admission cycle.

Which subjects are most important?

All subjects matter, but Physics, Chemistry, Botany, Zoology and MAT need different strategies. Biology rewards memory plus diagrams, Chemistry rewards reactions and calculations, Physics rewards formulas and concepts, and MAT rewards speed.

How should I use this page?

Start at the syllabus, tick the revision checklist, practice model questions, then run timed practice sets. Bookmark weak questions and review them before mocks.

Which books are recommended?

Use NEB Grade 11 and 12 textbooks as the base, then add a CEE-focused MCQ book or coaching material for speed practice. Official syllabus alignment matters more than collecting too many books.

How many mock tests should I take?

After completing the first syllabus pass, take one full mock per week. Near the exam, increase frequency only if you can still review mistakes properly.

Where do scholarship and admission details come from?

Scholarship, matching, seat and admission details should be checked from MEC notices and official college/admission documents for the current year.
