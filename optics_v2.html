Optics revision notes for CEE

Optics is one of the highest-yield physics chapters in the CEE syllabus because the same handful of ideas — reflection at plane and spherical mirrors, refraction at a plane surface, and total internal reflection — get recombined into dozens of question variations every year. Before attempting the timed sets below, it helps to lock down the sign convention, the mirror and lens formulas, and the conditions under which total internal reflection actually happens.

Ideas that show up almost every year

  • Mirror formula and magnification: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f, with magnification m = −v/u. Most CEE errors come from mixing up the sign convention rather than the formula itself.
  • Refraction at a plane surface: apparent depth is always less than real depth when going from a denser to a rarer medium, and the ratio real depth / apparent depth equals the refractive index.
  • Total internal reflection: only happens going from a denser to a rarer medium, and only when the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle. This single condition is tested from every angle — mirages, optical fibres, and prism-based instruments all trace back to it.
  • Lens power and combinations: power in dioptres is 1/f (in metres), and powers of thin lenses in contact simply add, which is why combination-lens numericals are usually faster than they look.

Where students usually lose marks

Sign convention slips (treating distances measured against the incident light direction as positive) and forgetting that a diverging lens or a convex mirror always gives a virtual, diminished image are the two most common mistakes in past CEE papers. Working through the timed sets below with the ray diagrams alongside each question is a fast way to build the instinct instead of memorising formulas in isolation.
