How the Saturday Model Set works

Each week's model set is a fresh 200-mark mixed paper built to mirror the real CEE exam: the same subject split across Physics, Chemistry, Botany, Zoology, and the Mental Ability Test (MAT), the same negative-marking pattern, and a similar difficulty curve to what has appeared in recent CEE papers. The goal isn't just answering questions in isolation — it's practising the full exam under time pressure, in one sitting, the way you'll actually have to on exam day.

Why a weekly full-length set helps

  • Pacing: a 200-mark paper forces you to budget time per subject the way the real exam will, instead of practising each subject separately without a clock.
  • Weak-topic detection: because the questions are mixed across all subjects, a bad week on one section shows up clearly instead of being hidden inside a single-subject quiz.
  • Leaderboard context: seeing where your score sits against everyone else who attempted the same set that week gives a more honest read on your standing than a score in isolation.

How to use the set well

Attempt it once, under real exam conditions and without pausing, before looking at any explanation. Afterward, review every wrong answer from the results screen — the point of a weekly set is less about the single score and more about spotting the same mistake before it costs marks in the actual exam.
